# Pricing, Commission & Settlement FAQ

Q: What is the wholesale price for admission tickets compared to the public retail price?
A: Partner agencies receive tickets at a wholesale rate that is typically 15-30% below the public retail price, depending on the venue and ticket tier. Exact net rates are listed in your agency's rate sheet.

Q: How is our commission calculated?
A: Commission is calculated as the difference between the wholesale net rate we invoice you and the retail price you charge the traveler. There is no separate commission deduction on top of the net rate — your margin is the spread.

Q: What currency are wholesale rates quoted in?
A: All wholesale rates are quoted in USD unless your agency agreement specifies a different settlement currency.

Q: When are invoices issued?
A: Invoices are issued weekly, every Monday, covering all confirmed bookings from the prior week.

Q: What is the payment due date after an invoice is issued?
A: Payment is due within 14 calendar days of the invoice date (net-14 terms), unless your agency agreement specifies different terms.

Q: What payment methods are accepted for settlement?
A: We accept bank wire transfer and major credit cards for settlement. Wire transfer is preferred for invoices above $5,000 due to card processing fees.

Q: Is there a minimum order volume required to maintain wholesale pricing?
A: No minimum order volume is required for the pilot program. Volume-based rate tiers may apply for agencies exceeding 500 tickets per month.

Q: Are there penalties for late payment?
A: Invoices unpaid after 30 days from the due date may incur a 1.5% monthly late fee and a temporary hold on new bookings until the balance is cleared.

Q: Do prices include local taxes or VAT?
A: Wholesale net rates are exclusive of any applicable local taxes or VAT. Taxes, where applicable, are itemized separately on the invoice.

Q: How do refunds affect settlement and commission?
A: When a ticket is refunded per the venue's refund policy, the wholesale amount is credited back on your next invoice. Any commission already realized on that booking is reversed in the same cycle.

Q: Can wholesale rates change without notice?
A: Wholesale rates are reviewed quarterly. Any rate change is communicated at least 14 days in advance and does not affect already-confirmed bookings.

Q: How do we get a copy of our current rate sheet?
A: Your current rate sheet is shared during onboarding and updated rate sheets are sent via email whenever rates change. Contact your account manager if you need a fresh copy.
